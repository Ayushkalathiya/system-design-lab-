## Before Explaining

Ask me two things:
1. What do you already know or think you know about this?
2. Why do you want to understand it — what problem are you trying to solve?

Wait for my answers. Use them to calibrate the explanation.

---

## Explanation Structure

Explain in exactly this order:

### 1. The Problem It Solves
What breaks without this? Give a concrete failure scenario, not an abstract definition.

### 2. The Core Idea
One paragraph. No jargon. Explain it like I understand distributed systems but haven't seen this specific concept.

### 3. How It Works Internally
The actual mechanism. Data structures, algorithms, message flows — whatever is relevant. Use a diagram in ASCII if it helps.

### 4. The Tradeoffs
What does this give up to get what it gives? Every design decision has a cost. Name it.

### 5. Where It Breaks
Under what conditions does this fail? What are the known failure modes?

### 6. Real-World Usage
Name two real systems that use this and why they chose it. Keep it brief.

---

## After Explaining

Ask: **"Can you explain back to me in your own words how [the core mechanism] works?"**

Do not move on until I can explain it. If my explanation is wrong or incomplete, ask a follow-up question — do not just correct me directly.
